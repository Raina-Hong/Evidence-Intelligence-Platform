# 02 Competitive Landscape: From Information Retrieval to Evidence Intelligence

## Executive Summary

The enterprise investigation market has entered the AI era.

From AI chat assistants to eDiscovery platforms and AI-powered investigation systems, vendors have significantly improved investigators' ability to search, retrieve and summarize information.

However, despite rapid advances in Large Language Models (LLMs) and agentic AI, today's investigation products remain fundamentally **search-centric**. Their primary objective is to help investigators access information faster—not to transform fragmented evidence into structured investigative knowledge.

This analysis argues that the next generation of investigation software is not another AI search tool. Instead, it is an **Evidence Intelligence Platform** that helps investigators organize evidence, construct knowledge and support human decision-making.

## 2. Competitive Landscape

The current investigation ecosystem can be grouped into four major categories.

### 2.1 AI Chat Assistants

**Representative Products**

- ChatGPT
- Claude
- Gemini
- Perplexity

#### Strengths

- Excellent natural language understanding
- Strong reasoning and summarization
- Flexible conversational interaction
- Fast information retrieval

#### Limitations

AI chat assistants excel at answering isolated questions.

However, they assume users already know what to ask and provide little support for long-running investigative workflows.

They lack persistent investigative memory, evidence provenance, relationship modeling and structured knowledge representation across multiple evidence sources.

As a result, they improve information retrieval but cannot effectively manage investigative knowledge.

---

### 2.2 eDiscovery Platforms

**Representative Products**

- Relativity aiR
- Everlaw
- DISCO

#### Strengths

- Large-scale document review
- AI-assisted legal discovery
- Email deduplication
- Document classification

#### Limitations

eDiscovery platforms are fundamentally document-centric.

Their objective is to help legal professionals review large collections of documents more efficiently.

Although generative AI has significantly accelerated document review, the investigation process still revolves around documents rather than evidence relationships.

In other words, these platforms optimize **document review** rather than **evidence reasoning**.

---

### 2.3 Investigation Management Platforms

**Representative Products**

- Thomson Reuters CLEAR Investigate
- NICE Actimize Investigation Suite
- IBM i2 Analyst's Notebook

#### Strengths

- Multi-source investigation workflow
- AI-powered investigation
- Source citation
- Audit trails
- Report generation

#### Limitations

Investigation management platforms represent the most advanced category in today's market.

Modern platforms have begun moving beyond simple search. For example, Thomson Reuters CLEAR Investigate introduces agentic AI capable of planning multi-step investigative workflows, searching across multiple data sources, generating reports and maintaining complete audit trails.

However, these systems still position AI primarily as an investigative assistant. Their primary objective is accelerating investigations rather than constructing an evolving knowledge representation of evidence.

Current paradigm:

```text
Question

↓

Agentic Search

↓

Evidence Collection

↓

Investigation Report
```

The workflow ends with information delivery rather than continuous knowledge construction.

---

### 2.4 Case Management Platforms

**Representative Products**

- CaseMap
- Clio
- Litify
- iManage

#### Strengths

- Centralized case management
- Document organization
- Workflow tracking
- Task management

#### Limitations

These platforms successfully organize investigative materials.

However, investigators are still responsible for manually establishing relationships between people, events and evidence.

They function as digital filing systems rather than intelligent knowledge systems.

---

## 3. Market Gap

Although these four categories solve different parts of the investigative workflow, they all share one common assumption:

**Information is the primary challenge.**

In reality, modern investigations rarely suffer from insufficient information.

Instead, investigators face an overwhelming amount of fragmented evidence.

Typical evidence sources include:

- Emails
- Chat messages
- Contracts
- Financial transactions
- Meeting transcripts
- Surveillance videos
- Mobile devices
- Public records

The challenge is no longer finding documents.

The challenge is understanding how disconnected pieces of evidence relate to one another.

Current products optimize search.

Few products optimize knowledge construction.

This represents the fundamental market opportunity.
